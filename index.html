Yesterday you bought a card for him, signed it with lies Yesterday he took you to dinner, he told you he loves you Yesterday you posted pictures of the flowers, the chocolates, the wine But I found you on my front door at the hour of lost souls today I know I should have been angry, closed the door in your face I am tired of being your skeleton, a dirty little secret Yet I stay, tethered to a life only lived behind closed curtains Is this love if it's a burden? Be thou bold, mine dear

I spy the longing that doth cloud thine eyes, whene’er thou steal’st a glance at me. Though thou hid’st thee behind a veil, thy thoughts lie plain as doth thy nose. I behold the curious fancies born within thy mind; I see them sail upon thine eyes, their engines fed by passion’s raging fire. Mine ears sit ready still to hear thy heart’s desire, thy voice proclaiming thy burning longing. Yet ever thou draw’st away, afeard— frighted, thou hold’st thy tongue in check. Give a voice unto the craving that doth haunt thee, For love is no true love if it be not brave; the heart is ne’er made whole whilst it concealeth its desire.
